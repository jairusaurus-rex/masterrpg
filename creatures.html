Creature Creation

Other than other humans, you can run into animals and all sorts of creatures. Have a creature in mind, create them with the below choices. Give a creature a threat to determine how tough it is then buy options for that creature with creature points.

Creatures only have an action, move, and reaction. Also, once their hit points reach 0 or below, it dies.

Stats

A creature does not roll nor have skills. They are rolled against by players. A creature has a set Difficulty Challenge or DC. player rolls to hit a creature and rolls to not be hit by the creature. Both are against the DC. The creatures perception, bravery, and other skills are run by that same DC. If a creature is unable to do something due to its nature, its DC is nulled or in this case, -5

DC is 10 plus Threat Level

A creature has  hit points of 5 for every threat level.

A create has 20 feet speed plus 1 for every threat level rounded up to the nearest 5

A creature deals 5 plus threat level damage.

The total buff/debuff points cannot be more than the Threat level or less than negative than 5

For each size larger than small, the hit points increase by 5, speed increase by 5, damage increase by 1, CD decrease by 1.

A creature can only be so large every threat level. The creature can always be smaller than that regardless of threat level

Quick builds

If you know the threat level and size modifier, you can quick calculate the stats of your creature

Hit points = 5 +  (threat level * 5) + (size modifier * 5)

Speed = Round up to nearest 5 (20 + threat level + size modifier)

DC = 15 + threat level - size modifier

Damage = 5 threat level + size modifier

Creature points = threat level

Creature options

Book 1: Core

You get a creature point for each threat level, minimum of 0. Use these points to give a creature a special option.

If there is something with the tag, “continues”, you can gain that option multiple times

Name

Description

Attach

When making a successful attack, if the target was at least one size larger than the creature, the creature automatically grapples the target.

Blind Sight (continuous)

Each time a creature gain this, you gain 10 ft of blind sightCreature can use other senses to ‘see’. They cannot be blinded for this range nor can someone be stealthed in their range

Burrow (continuous)

The creature can dig through the earth at full speed. Every time this is gained, its digging speed increases by 5 ft

Climb (continuous)

The creature can climb at full speed. Every time this is gained, its climb speed increases by 5 ft

Contagion

If the creature makes a successful attack, the enemy must make a fortitude vs creatures attack. If the creature wins, the enemy gains the condition of Dease. The challenge of the dease is the creature’s attack

Dark vision (continuous)

Each time a creature gains this, it gains 50 ft of dark vision

Deep breath

(continuous)

Each time a creature gains this, it can hold its breath for another hour

Defensive abilities

When Attacked, the creature can use its reaction to attack back for half its damage

Fast (continuous)

Each time a creature gains this, it gains 5 feet of speed

Fly (continuous)

If each times a creature gains this is equal or grater than the size modifier, the creature can fly at its speed. Otherwise it flies at half its speed

Gills

Creature can breath underwater but cannot move on land

Grab

For its action, a creature can attack and grapple at the same time.

Multi-attack

Creature can attack twice for its action

Poisoned Death

Creature can poison an enemy after a successful attack. At the poisoned enemy turn, it makes a fortitude vs the creature's DC until the poisoned enemy wins. If the creature wins, poisoned enemy takes one level of exhaustion. Once the poisoned enemy wins, they are no longer poisoned

Poison Paradise

Creature can poison an enemy after a successful attack. At the poisoned enemy turn, it makes a fortitude vs the creature's attack until the poisoned enemy wins. If the creature wins, poisoned enemy falls down and cannot move. The poisoned enemy becomes helpless until the poisoned enemy wins, once it wins, it is no longer poisoned.

Pounce

For its action, a creature can move half its speed in a straight line and attack at the same time.

Pull

When a creature makes a ranged attack, that creature can make a grapple check for free to bring the target their size or small closer to itself up to melee range

Prey

A creature cannot attack nor do damage but their DC increases by 2 and speed by 5

Projectile

A creature has an attack with range of 100 feet but can only do half damage rounded down

Ranged Attack (continuous)

Each time  creature gains this option, they gain 5 feet of a ranged attack

Size larger

The creature can be one size larger than allowed.

Shield

Creature can use a reaction to force an enemy that is attacking it to reroll the attack

Squirmy

(continuous

Each time  creature gains this option, they lose 10 hit points but their DC increase by 1

Stench

(continuous)

Creatures have a stench around them. Whenever an enemy starts their turn in it, they must make a fortitude vs creatures attack. If they lose, they lose half their speed and has a  debuff to all checks. The amount of debuffs equals the number of times a creature took this option. The distance the stench circles the creature is 15 ft for every time the creature took this option.

Study (continuous)

Each time creature gains this option, their hit points increase by 2

Swarm

The creature automatically hits all attacks, though it’s damage is halved rounded down (min 1). When the creature is at half health or lower, it’s damage is halved again (rounded down)

Swim (continuous)

The creature can swim at full speed. Every time this is gained, its swim speed increases by 5 ft

Thick skin

Each time creature gains this option, they gain 1 resistance to weapons and strikes

Trample

For its action, a creature can move and run through any enemy smaller than itself. As it passes, it makes a free attack.

Trip

For its action, a creature can attack and grapple at the same time, if successful, the target is tripped and becomes Prone instead of grappled.

Companion Animal Tricks

Companion animals are controlled by the Game Master, not the player. The player can, however, order the animal around if the animal knows any tricks. The companion takes its turn on the same time as the player. Tricks are listed below

Name

description

Attack

Companion will attack a particular target until target is dead

Capture

Companion will try to pin a target

Defend

Companion will try to stay near target you point out, it will attack anything threatening that target

Fetch

Companion will try to bring you an object that you point out to it

Fight

Companion will keep attacking. It sees anything fighting that it has not seen before or anything attacking you or itself as an enemy. Starting with the closest enemy, it will keep fighting until everything is dead

Go

Companion will go to a location that you point out and try to remain there

Guard

Companion will run to a target you point out. If target runs or fights, Companion will attack it until the target remains still and does not attack or dies. Then the companion will just stay near the target until the target runs or fights again

Heel

Companion will stop current action and try to remain where it is

Mount

Companion will allow you to ride itself, you both take your move actions together and you follow the mount rules for combat.

Return

Companion will try to stay by your side or attack the target you are attacking until target is dead or you attack something else

Track

Given a sent or trail, the companion will aid you in tracking down a target. You gain a buff to Tracking equal to the Threat level  your companion has

Watch

Companion will move to a location you point out and keep watch for harmful creatures or people it does not recognize. It will make noise to alert you

Mounted Combat

You can only mount something one size larger and willing.

It takes a Move Action to mount a creature and a Minor Action to dismount. For free you can fall prone off your Mount.

During combat, if you are riding a non-companion creature or a non-war trained mount, you must make a ride check vs 20 at the start of your turn. If you fail, your ride will not move.

While mounted, you gain a buff on all weapon skill checks on all enemies your size or smaller that are not mounted.

You move at your mounts speed.

You may get knocked off your horse when taking damage. This happens if one of the following is true, your mount does not have a saddle or your take damage that leaves you with negative health. You must make a ride check vs the amount of damage you took. If you lose, you are knocked off your ride and fall prone.
