Every character has a number of feat tiers (or levels) equal to their bonus plus their level. When leveling up, fill this number in one of two ways.

Every time you level up you must choose new feats equal to that number. To get a feat, you must first meet all the prerequisites it requires. This new feat counts as a tier one and just adds one to your total of feat tiers for the above calculation.

Instead of choosing a new feat, you can instead improve a feat you already have. You can do this if a feat has tiers. When you get a feat with tiers for the first time, you get that feat at tier 1. You can improve your tier anytime you get a new feat by instead choosing to go one more tier up on a feat you already have. You must meet the prerequisites for that tier to do so, just like selecting a feat.
