Anything you do that requires  roll is a Skill Check. This includes attack and defending checks. There are 5 numbers calculated in a check that is added to a 20 sided dice. However, most of these skills only require one ability score added to a d20. Therefore you should only write done skills that you have changed to proficiencies and feats. Your GM will announce skills so you should have to remember all of the ones you have not altered this way. For example, for an intimidate check, the GM will say “Roll Intimate or Grit” If you didn’t alter intimate, you won’t have written in on your sheet and would just roll a d20 and add your Grit.

There are 5 things a skill has to add to your d20, Primary ability, secondary ability, Proficiency, Total buff points, and today debuff points (The last one is subtracted from the roll.)

Primary  Ability

At the very least, every skill has a primary ability score. Each has a defaulted ability score but you can change it through feats. You cannot have more than one ability score be a primary ability score

Secondary Ability

You can add a secondary ability score to a skill through feats. If you have more than one feat that gives a skill a secondary ability score, you must choose one and only one of those secondary ability scores. You cannot have a skill with more than one secondary ability score.

Proficiency

Proficiencies are skills that your character is proficient in. Every character has proficiencies equal to their intelligence, wit, charisma, bonus plus five. This number can also increase due to some feats. Every time you level up you must choose new proficiency equal to that number.

If you have a skill with a proficiency, you add your character bonus to that skill roll.

Buff  or Debuff

Buffs and Debuffs are situational or feat given additions or subtractions to a skill check. The greatest, or worst, part is, unlike the rest, they stack on each other. Everything that gives you a buff is added together and put in your skill check. But everything that gives you a debuff is added together then is minused to your skill check.

Your total between buff and debuff cannot be greater than your bonus or less than your negative 5.

Nulled!

When a skill is nulled, usually due to some effect or due to using a skill improperly, That skill cannot use the proficiency or secondary ability, even if they are attached to the skill. Instead, just count the primary plus the buffs and debuffs.

Creatures that have skilled that are nulled take 5 debuffs instead
