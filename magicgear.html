Enchanting Rules

Worthiness

You can find magical items your Game Master has created or you can try to enchant. Even though you may have a magic item equipped or worn, the magic still will not apply to you or be used by you. You must first attune the item to you. This takes ten minutes for every magic power the time has. This item remains attuned to you unless you remove it or someone else has the item and attunes it to themselves. You can remove the attunement yourself at half the time. You can also spend an hour to attune or un-attune as many items as you are able.

You can attune only a limited number of items, armor, and weapons. You have a worthy level of *A plus your bonus. The total number of the magic power of all attuned items cannot exceed your worthy level.

Enchanted Items

An item can have multiple enchantments to it, each adding to its magic power.

Some enchantments can only be put on certain items listed

Each enchantment has a magic power that is added to an item. Some are listed as tiered. Tiered items the effect of the enchantment. The number of the tier is the amount added to the magic power. You cannot get higher than 5 on tiered items

No item stacks with another item and items cannot have the same enchantment twice, even if you make a different option with that enchantment. If you have more than one item attune with the same effect, you can only use one of those effects. Exceptions to this include enchantment that have different options (like different buffs to two different skills) or skills that need an item to use (like weapons, shields, and armor) With skills that require items, you must use the item enchanted to gain the effects of that enchantment. For instance, a sword that uses fire damage must be used for that fire damage to take effect, other weapons do not gain that fire damage.

You can add more than one enchantment to one item. This is more useful to armor and weapons. You cannot have an item with more than 10 magic power.

Some enchantments need to be placed on weapons or armor or shields and other can be put on any item. Any times must either follow two criterias. They can either be an item that takes a large carry load, or a small item that can be worn like rings or amulets. Unenchanted, they cost one gold and have one small carry load. You can wear one headpiece, one pair of gloves, one pair of boots and as many rings, amulets, bracelets, or belts you want. Enchantments that are article of clothing must be worn and attuned before the enchantments effect can take place

The cost of enchanted items are the spell level times 1000.
