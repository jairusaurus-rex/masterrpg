Spell Rules

Casting Spells

Mana. Having any magic tier gives you magic mana. The amount of mana you have is equal to *A + Bonus + Total Magic Tiers. Total Magic Tier includes all tiers from magic feats.

Spending Mana. When you cast a spell, you spend an amount of mana equal to the Spell’s level.

Running out of mana. When you run out of mana, you can still cast spells but at a deeper cost. When you can spells that uses more mana that you use, your mana points will go into the negative. Anytime you cast a spell and have negative spell points, two things happen. First you lose hitpoints equal to the absolute value of your negative mana points, and gain a level of exhaustion.

For example, If you have five mana then cast a fourth level spell, your mana becomes one. The next turn, if you cast the same level four spell, your mana will be negative three, you take three damage and gain an exhaustion level. The next turn, if you cast the same level four spell, your mana will be negative seven, you take seven damage and gain another exhaustion level.

Concentration. You can only cast one spell with a duration that has concentration at a time. Anytime you want to cast another one, you must release the previous one.

When you have a spell with concentration running and take damage, you must make a concentration check vs the damage. If you fail, your spell ends.

If you have 0 or less hit points, your spell with concentration ends

Reactions. When someone casts a spell within 5 feet of you, you can use an attack of opportunity.

If you have 0 or less hit points, your spell with concentration ends

Casting Magic

Spell Tiers

The retirement for any tier is the tier equal to your *A (=*A). There are five types of magic tiers. You must select each one as a separate tier when picking your tiers. You must upgrade each magic type separately as well. Each magic type counts as their own feat: Arcane Black Elemental Holy and Nature

Spells Known

Having any Spells Known gives you a number of spells you know. This number is equal to *I + Bonus + Total Magic Tiers. Total Magic Tier includes all tiers from magic feats

There are 5 types of magic. You can only add spells to Spells Known if you have Tier in that magic type: Arcane Black Elemental Holy and Nature

You can only add a spell to spells Known if the spell level is equal to or less than the tier you have.

You do not have to fill spells known right away. You can add spells anytime as long as you haven’t filled the list. It takes one minute per spell level to add a spell to spells known

Types of Damage

Elemental: Acid Cold Electric Fire Pressure and Sonic

Non Elemental: Necrotic and Psychic

Spell Creation

Upgrades

All spells have a starting level. Each spell can be upgraded to a new spell. The Spell Level of that new spell equals the starting level plus how many upgrades the spell has. Some upgrades require you to select the previous upgrades. You can tell by the way it is listed.

This is a new spell added to your Spells Known. This spell also requires a new name.

Two Upgrades all spells have are Silent and motionless. Since all spells require you to speak and move your hand. Silent removes the sonic component and motionless lets removes the motion portion.

Upgrades with the ‘+’ symbol can be taken as many times as you want.

Spell Range Type

All spells have one range type. When you upgrade to a new range type, you remove the previous version. Each range type has its own upgrades you can add.

If harmful, each range type has its own way of attacking and requires the attacker and defender to roll certain skill checks. When the defender wins, they either do not take effect (negate) or takes half damage if damage is dealt (half). If helpful, it has a skill check vs a different number
