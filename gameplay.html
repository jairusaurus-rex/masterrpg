Death
  • If any character has negative hit points that are greater (in absolute value) to their max hit points, they are dead.
  • If any character has more death points than their stamina points, they are dead.
  • If any character’s stamina is lower than 0, they are dead

Action Economy

Spent and Ready action: During combat, every player gets four actions to use how they seem fit: Major, Move, Minor, and Reaction. These either have the status of Spent or Ready. Once one is Spent, it is considered Spent until the start of your turn therefore you cannot use them again.

At the start of combat, reaction is considered Ready and the rest are considered Spent. At the start of your turn, unless you are disoriented, all actions are turned to Ready. This is the VERY FIRST thing that happens in your turn.  

Except to react, your actions must be used on your turn. You can use any of your actions in any order unless specified by the action act. You must show what action is Spent for what action act. To keep track of this, we recommend using cards and flipping them over when Spent.

Action Rankings: Actions have ranking. A higher ranked actions can be spent on a lower one. When doing this, the higher ranked one is still used and still only uses one thing on the lower ranked action list. This is trading down and is at the players choice. For example, you can move with your move action then move again using your major action, therefore doubling your speed that turn.

You cannot trade up in actions. For example, a move action cannot be spent on a major action. However, you can gain feats that would lower an act to a lower rank action.

The ranking goes as follows. A major action is the top rank action followed by the move action followed by the minor action followed by reaction.

Movement: Your character has a speed movement. Whenever you move at your speed, always round up to the nearest 5. If your speed is 22, you can treat that speed as though you can move 25 feet.

If anything halves your speed, have your actual speed first then round up to the nearest 5. If your speed is 22 and you must half you speed for any reason, your speed is now 11 and you can treat that half speed as 15 feet.

Reaction triggers: reactions act can only be used when something triggers the act. Even if you can trade down and use multiple reactions, you can still only use one reaction per trigger. See reaction descriptions for more details.

Major Actions

Standard Attack: Use a weapon or natural ability to use an attack. You can do this once per turn and only with a Major Action. This is called a Standard Attack for all feats that use Standard Attacks. All other attacks are called Extra Attacks. When doing this with a weapon, if the weapon is a single handed weapon, you must have it in your main hand.

Disengage: You can Disengage on your turn. If you do, others cannot make an attack of opportunity on that turn.

Heal Other: If another ally is at negative hit points and is within reach, you can perform a Heal Other on him. You must make a Medicine check against the number of negative hit points. If successful, that ally's hit points return to 0. If unsuccessful, that ally takes one damage.

Grapple: If an enemy is within reach you can attempt to make a grapple check against the enemies grapple check. If successful you can do one of the following. Put the enemy in the Grappled condition. Escape the grappled condition. If the enemy is already in the grappled condition, put them in the pinned condition.  If you are in the pinned condition, escape to the grappled position.

You can only grapple something your size or smaller, though you can attempt escape anyone that grappled or pinned you.

Grapple, while grappling: You can only grapple or pin one enemy at a time. Your enemy cannot have both conditions grappled and pinned.If you are grappling someone or pinning someone, you can let them go for a free action. If you have someone pinned, you can put them in the grapple condition instead for a free action.  If you are grappling someone or pinning someone, you can drag them with you half speed but you cannot make them fall down something without you. If you have someone pinned, you cannot use a Standard Action until they are released.

Fix Weapon: If your weapon has a broken condition, you can Fix Weapon to take the broken condition away from the weapon.

Drink. You can drink any remedy or potion or you can force an unconscious person to drink a potion.

Move Action

Move: You can move to your full speed. If you have more than one move speed, such as fly, you can start to move in the other way by subtracting what you have already moved.

Rise up: If you are knocked to the ground, you can use your move action to your feet.

Reposition: If you are in Melee combat, you can move 5 feet in any direction as long as you stay adjacent to one of the enemies you were in melee combat with. No one can us Attack of Opportunity for this move action.

Leap: You can jump at *S + *D feet forward in a straight line. You can jump *S feet up.

Minor Action

Dash: You can move up to your speed in a perfect line. You can only move in one way (ground, swimming, etc.) You cannot do this action while wearing armor

Dash: You can move up to your speed in a perfect line. You can only move in one way (ground, swimming, etc.) You cannot do this action while wearing armor

Pickup object: Pick up any object off the floor

interact with object: Use or open or act with any minor object.

Fall: You take the time to fall to the ground and make yourself prone. You can also make a deception skill check against anyone’s insight. If you win, you can fool someone to think you have fallen in combat.

Off hand attack: After making a standard attack with your main hand, you can make an extra attack with your off hand if it is a light weapon. The skill with your off hand is nulled. That weapon does one less damage than it normally would

Fall: You take the time to fall to the ground and make yourself prone. You can also make a deception skill check against anyone’s insight. If you win, you can fool someone to think you have fallen in combat.

Order trick: Order your companion animal to do a trick or follow an order

Give item: you can give an item a willing character within 5 feet of you or throw something 1 large weight or smaller to a willing character within 5 times your *S.

Reaction

Any time you can make a reaction, even if you have more than one action to use, you can use only ONE reaction. For example, if someone attacks you, you can only use one reaction for that attack before and after. You can only choose between things like shield, redirect shot, or parry. For example, you can only do one attack of opportunity for each event that triggered the attack of opportunity.

Attack of opportunity: You can make an extra attack if someone moves LEAVE any five feet space that is adjacent to you. You can also make the attack of opportunity if you are adjacent to anyone who does one of the following: Grapple, drink, heal other, hide, fix weapon, Rise up, sheath weapon or pickup object.

Raise shield: If a weapon attacks you and you fail to defend against it., you can choose to raise your shield before making a defense check. You use your Shield Skill Check against the same weapon skill check roll. If successful, you don’t have to make the defense check.

Raise shield: If a weapon attacks you and you fail to defend against it., you can choose to raise your shield before making a defense check. You use your Shield Skill Check against the same weapon skill check roll. If successful, you don’t have to make the defense check.

Aid Other:  If an ally adjacent to you is attempting a Skill check in a skill you are proficient in, you can make an Aid Other act. The ally gains a buff.  

Free Action

Some actions do not take any use of an action. You can drop anything in your hands at any time on your turn. You can also talk for six seconds on your turn. You can also freely take any item given to you if you have enough free hands to do so.

Combat

Attacking and Damage

Players and enemies react the same way unless specified. Rules applying to players apply to enemies and vise versa.

Initiative:  All players and creatures roll initiative and follow their turns in that order.

Attacking: When attacking, make the check assigned on the weapon. The enemy must make a defense move. You can only attack with a weapon you have in hand. You can Strike with your hands or feet or any other natural attack.

Defense: When making a defense move, choose either Dodge or Armor Skill check. The attacker must beat that check with their attack to do damage.

An attacker can force the roll if an act states directly that you must roll a dodge or must roll an armor, you must roll that skill check.

If you make an armor check without wearing armor, your check is nulled.

Damage: If the attack is successful, you deal the damage listed in the weapon along with any benefit given by feats. Unless one has damage immunity, damage always does at least one point.

Attacking what you cannot see. You can only attack a target that you know is there. Stealthed creatures cannot be targeted. If an enemy is not stealthed but is invisible or in darkness, you can still attempt an attack. If the enemy made a noise or an attack, you can make an attack or cast a spell targeting the same location. If the enemy moved since then, the attack failed. If it is an attack or a spell that targets an enemy, you can make the skill check nulled.

Damage resistance: When dealt damage with a type that you have resistance, you reduce that damage by the resistance number. You must take at least one damage.

Damage volubility: When dealt damage with a type you are valuable to, you take that damage plus half that damage.  

Damage Immunity: When dealt damage with a type you are Immune to, you take no damage.

Situational buffs: You can gain a buff for fighting in a particular way. You can gain a buff for coming out of stealth, flanking an enemy by being on the other side of the enemy that an ally is, or having the higher ground.

Surprised Enemies: If any enemy starts a fight where the enemy either does not know where your party is or does not expect you would attack, that enemy is disoriented at the beginning of the fight.

Stealth. If an enemy does not notice your character at the start of the fight, you are stealthed to that enemy. Any weapon or strike attack against that enemy forces that enemy to use the armor skill, not dodge skill. Your attack can use *D or *W as your secondary ability for your attack skill.

If you are stealthed to all enemies, you can use your stealth skill check for initiative instead of your initiative skill checks.

During a fight, if the enemy knows you are there or has seen you once, you cannot stealth upon them, even if they suddenly don’t see you. They are treated as though they are expecting you

Creatures on high alert cannot be stealthed upon. If guards are doing their daily routine, they are on alert but guards that expect an attack are on high alert.

You move at half speed while stealthed

Below 0 Hit Points and Dying

Below 0 hit points: After taking damage, if you end up with negative health, there are a few effects that trigger against your character.

Below negative stamina: If your health more has negative hit points (in absolute value) than your stamina, ALL your skills are nulled. You may roll your concentration check and stabilize check before this takes place.

Below 0 hit points concentration: Any damage that takes you below 0, or further under 0 hit points, gives you the Dying condition.

If you are conscious, this also forces you to make a concentration check equal to 10 plus the damage dealt. If you fail, you gain the unconscious condition.

Dying Condition: whether conscious or not, if your character has a dying condition, at the beginning of your turn you must make a stabilization skill check. You must make a 10 plus your negative hit points or above.  If successful, you remove the Dying condition, if failed, you gain one death point.

Any time you are healed to 0 or higher hit points, you remove the Dying condition

Gaining consciousness: If you were knocked unconscious by going below 0 hit points, you can gain consciousness if you recover back to 0 or higher hit points. At the start or your turn you can roll Will Power Skill Check to get over 20. If successful, you remove the unconscious condition and gain the disoriented condition. You are still prone.

Non-lethal damage: Any time you are attacking with a melee weapon or a strike, you can choose to do non-Lethal damage. If the attack check is nulled. If the target gets below 0 hit points, they do not gain the Dying condition. Their concentration check to stay conscious is double the damage rather than just the damage. They can still die if their negative hit points are too great. See Death.

Death

If any character has negative hit points that are greater (in absolute value) to their max hit points, they are dead.

If any character has more death points than their stamina points, they are dead.

If any character’s stamina is lower than 0, they are dead

Persistent Conditions

Any condition that is persistent must be rolled once and that number sticks with the person with the condition, that number is written besides the condition. At the start of their turn, they can try to again use their skill, they same skill they used to try to avoid that condition, against the number next to the condition. If successful, you can remove the condition, if failed, the number next to the condition decreases by one and you still have the condition

Adventuring

Surviving

Falling damage: If the fall is 20 feet or more, make a reflex save vs the amount of feet divided by 10. On success, take damage equal to the fall feet divided by 20, On failure, take damage equal to the fall feet divided by 10 and are prone. Additionally, for every 10 feet above 50 feet, gain one death point no matter if you succeed or fail.

Suffocating: You can hold your breath for turns up to your Stamina Points. Each turn after that amount of time gives you a death point.

Dehydration: After one day of no water, you gain the Sick condition until you drink water. Each day without water gives you an exhausted level and a death point.

Starvation: After one day of no food, you gain the Sick condition until you eat food. Each day without food gives you an exhausted level.

Sleep Deprivation: Each day (or each 24 hour period) without rest gives you an exhausted level. Each day (or 24 hour period) after a combat, you need to make a Willpower Skill Check of 10 plus the number of days you have stayed awake. If you fail, your character falls asleep. Unless your character is awakened by another creature, you stay asleep for 8 hours.

Climate: Comes weather and climate can be detrimental to you. Weather can be too cold or too hot. The harshness of cold or hot climate are marked by degrees of danger. Each degree increases the threat to your life.

Climate: Comes weather and climate can be detrimental to you. Weather can be too cold or too hot. The harshness of cold or hot climate are marked by degrees of danger. Each degree increases the threat to your life.

Degrees are reduced by 1 for drinking to portions of water and 1 for being in the shade. Being in water, except heated water, reduces the degree by 2  or walking around in wet clothes reduces the degree by 1.

Dome climates, like a desert, can change at different times of the day. It might be hot in the day and cold at night.

Sight

Sight  You can see 2 miles away. You can see 1 mile away in the rain. Fog can limit sight by 500 or 100.

Dark vision. In the dark, you can see a number of feet equal to your darkvision. In Dim light, you can see a number of feet equal twice your darkvision

Advanced Darkness. Advanced Darkness is magic based. Dark vision cannot affect it. You can see 0 feet in Advanced darkness

Carry Load

You can carry only so many large items. Some large items increase your ability to carry small items. You can also carry a large item with the weight of one in your small carry load for 5 small weights.

Overhead and Dragging:  You can lift over your head 100lbs * S and drag or push 500lbs * S

Overburden: If you carry more than your load allows (2S + G + Bonus), you are overburden.

Overencumbered If you carry more than twice your load allows (2 *(2S + G + Bonus)), you are Overencumbered.

Small load rules: You cannot carry more than your Small load allows because you would drop things.

Rest

Full 8 hours. A rest is an 8 hour period where your character is sleeping. You must remain sleeping for the full 8 hours.

Recovery. Sleeping for 8 hours prevents Sleep Deprivation. You also regain hit points, regain mana, and remove some negative effects. The list of things that happen after the 8 hour rest if found below

  • You regain hit points equal to your stamina until it reaches your total hit points. You cannot recover over your total hit points.
  • You may remove one level of exhaustion. If you only have one level of exhaustion, you can remove that condition
  • If you have no levels of exhaustion, you can remove one death point.
  • If any feat has a number of uses that allows you to regain them on a rest, you regain all of those but cannot exceed the max number

Standing Guard. An exemption is staying awake to stand guard. A you can stand guard for up to two hours as long as you rest after the same amount and your total time asleep must be 8 hours.

For example if you slept while someone was sleeping but was awake for your shift of 2 hours, you must rest another 2 hours before being awakened. Then you take another shift of 1 hour, you must rest 1 hour before being awake. Since you guarded a total of 3 hours, to get the effects of the full rest, you had to have slept for 8 hours. The total time past then would be 11 hours.

Resting again. After you gain the full effects of a rest, you cannot start to rest again until 8 hours has passed.

Traveling

You can travel by foot or mount 20 miles a day. This takes 8 hours or you can travel 5 miles an hour. Any additional mile past 20 gives you one level of exhaustion and every 5 miles after that gives you yet another level of exhaustion.

Any vehicle gives you the speed they can travel in 12 hours. A number of crew must keep it going during that time. Every hour a character works on the vehicle after that gains one level of exhaustion.
